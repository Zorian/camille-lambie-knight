Art Story
Selected Works · Commissions

Artwork that resonates long after you’ve left the room.

Working primarily in oil, I create emotionally driven portraiture exploring transformation, resilience and human connection. Combining greyscale with selective colour, my work reflects the tension between stillness and movement, light and shadow — using motifs such as butterflies and elements from nature to explore growth, healing and hope.

Alongside my personal practice, I create commissioned works for private and corporate collectors internationally — including Premier League footballers, Olympians and leading figures in music. Notable projects include a large-scale tribute to Jamal Edwards MBE for 0207 Def Jam, permanently displayed at their London headquarters.

Alongside my work as an artist, I build relationships that connect people, creativity and culture across the arts, music, business and community sectors. I currently serve as UK Lead for Regime Music Société (RMS), developing creative partnerships and cultural initiatives across the UK.

Lived experience · Recovery · Hope

Art entered my life during one of its most difficult chapters.

As a teenager, I spent several years in and out of hospital receiving treatment for anorexia nervosa. During that time I experienced first-hand the challenges of living with mental illness — but I also discovered something that would shape the course of my life forever: painting.

What began as a way to process emotion and navigate recovery gradually became a way of understanding myself, communicating with others and finding hope beyond the hospital walls. Art gave me a sense of purpose at a time when I had very little belief in myself, and it remains the foundation of everything I do today.

Those experiences shaped not only my artwork, but the way I approach people, collaboration and community. They taught me the value of resilience, empathy and human connection.

Full circle
Seeing the finished resource used across the service was a meaningful full-circle moment.

Since recovering, I’ve returned to my former hospital and outpatient units to share my experience with young people and healthcare professionals. More recently I collaborated with my former outpatient team at The Maudsley to redesign their family-therapy information leaflet — creating the artwork, layout, colour palette and visual identity.

It reinforced my belief in the power of lived experience, and what creativity can do for recovery, connection and positive change.
